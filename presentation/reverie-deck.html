The two networks
One product, two questions.

Model 1 · GRU

"What comes next in your sequence?"
  • Recurrent network (RNN)
  • Sequential, order matters
  • Classification: softmax over the catalog
  • Trained on MovieLens

Model 2 · NCF

"How much will you like this film?"
  • Feed-forward network (ANN) with embeddings
  • Collaborative, learns from everyone
  • Regression: a predicted rating 1–10
  • Trained on 9.9M Letterboxd ratings

Serving · the real recommender
We rank by your nearest favorites.
A cold fold-in underperformed, so the app ranks each film by its similarity to your highest-rated films in the learned movie-embedding space, plus a genre anchor and a recency nudge.
A choice we reached by testing: the embedding space is well trained; a fresh user vector from a few ratings is not.

Reflection

Lessons learned

  • Collaborative signal beats content; a single profile overfits
  • "Drama" tags half of all films, rank by nearest favorites
  • Honest baselines matter more than one flashy number

Known issues

  • Cold start: a brand-new viewer sits near their average
  • Embeddings lean to a Letterboxd cinephile cluster
  • Posters hotlink the Letterboxd CDN, a fragile dependency

Next steps

  • Per-user fine-tuning as a history grows
  • A mainstream-vs-deep-cuts dial
  • Group Blend beyond two people
